1. The Task Paralysis Shatterer
"I need to do [task] but I can't start. Don't give me a to-do list. Give me only ONE physical action I can do in the next 90 seconds that requires zero decisions. Then stop."

2. The Dopamine Menu Architect
"Build me a 'dopamine menu' for my work session on [project]. Give me 3 'main dishes' (deep work tasks), 3 'sides' (easy wins), and 2 'desserts' (rewarding tasks I actually enjoy). I pick what I'm drawn to in the moment."

3. The Body Double Simulator
"Be my body double for the next 30 minutes. I'm working on [task]. Every time I message you, just respond with: what I said I'd do, a one-line check-in, and one micro-encouragement. No long responses. Keep me anchored."

4. The Hyperfocus Activator
"Turn [boring task] into a challenge or game. Give it stakes, a time limit, and a scoring system. Make it feel like something I'd actually want to win. Be creative — my brain needs a reason to care."

5. The Time Blindness Corrector
"I have [X hours] to do [task list]. I have no sense of time. Create a session map that anchors each task to a real-world time (e.g. 'at 10:15, not in 20 minutes'). Add a buffer zone and one emergency reset slot."

6. The Transition Bridge
"I just finished [task A] and need to move to [task B] but I'm stuck in the gap. Write me a 3-step transition ritual — under 2 minutes — that closes the first task mentally and opens the second one. Make it physical, specific, and simple."

7. The Shame Spiral Interrupter
"I haven't worked on [task] in [time period] and I feel awful about it. Don't motivate me. Don't lecture me. Just help me figure out the smallest re-entry point that doesn't require me to 'catch up' first. Treat it like I'm starting fresh today."

8. The End-of-Day Debrief
"Help me close today. Ask me 4 quick questions — one about what I actually finished, one about what's still open, one about what's blocking me tomorrow, and one about one small win I can acknowledge. Then summarize it as a clean 'brain dump' I can read tomorrow morning to restart fast."

The prompts shared in this post are creative productivity tools designed to help structure thinking and reduce overwhelm. They are not a medical treatment, therapy, or clinical intervention for ADHD or any other condition.
AI cannot diagnose, treat, or replace professional support. If you or someone you know is struggling with ADHD, please consult a qualified healthcare professional or licensed therapist.
Results vary from person to person. What works for one brain may not work for another and that's completely okay.
Use these as a starting point, not a solution.

1/ Curriculum Architect Prompt
Use for: Building a full learning path

Act as a world-class curriculum designer.
I want to master [skill] in [timeframe] starting from [current level].
Create a week-by-week study plan with:
1. Learning objectives per week
2. Core resources: books, papers, courses, docs
3. Practice projects with increasing difficulty
4. How to measure progress
5. Common pitfalls to avoid
Make it realistic for [X hours/week].
-------------
2/ Spaced Repetition Card Maker
Use for: Retaining what you learn

Code
From this text/topic: [paste notes or topic]
Create Anki-style flashcards using active recall + close deletion.
Focus on: concepts I must derive, not memorize; common bugs; trade-offs.
Format as Q:... A:...
Generate 15 cards, ordered by importance.
-------------
3/ Transfer Learning Prompt
Use for: Applying skill A to domain B

Code
I know [skill A]. I want to learn [skill B].
Map concepts from A onto B to accelerate learning.
Show me: what transfers directly, what’s analogous but different, what’s completely new.
Then give me 3 exercises that use my A skills to bootstrap B.
