## Educational tools and lessons for children with special needs

Supporting children with special needs requires a personalized, multisensory approach that prioritizes individual goals, clear structure, and the removal of barriers to learning.

-Essential Assistive Technologies (AT)

Assistive technology can bridge the gap between a student's ability and the demands of the curriculum.

--Graphic Organizers: Digital mind-mapping tools help students break complex tasks into smaller, manageable chunks

--Explicit Instruction: Break learning into small, logical steps. Model the task, provide guided practice, and then move to independent practice.  

--Visual Supports: Use visual schedules (e.g., picture cards) to make the day predictable and reduce anxiety, especially for students with autism.  

--Scaffolding: Provide heavy support initially and gradually remove it as the student gains mastery.
